Reflection-sheet

This is the mind map to summarize the reflection sheet of the journal club

  • The questions were largely designed based on the peer-review process
  • I strongly recommend you to take a look at any given peer review guideline: nature.com/nature/for-referees/policies-and-processes and the open review document in some journals (e.g., Nature Communication). They are usually attached in their online contents.

Research questions?

In your own words, provide a brief description of the main research question being pursued in this paper.

  • Will the present data fill critical knowledge gap in a particular area?
  • Is the study innovative and provoke a broad interest in a given field?

Methods & data

Consider the methods used and the data presented. Do the data support the hypothesis/research question? Explain why or why not. Were there any unexpected results?

  • Are there flaws in the methods?
  • Sample number is described and good enough for solid statistic inference? Did they perform repeated experiment if necessary?

authors’ interpretation

Do you agree with the authors’ interpretation of the data? Why or why not?

  • Is the interpretation of the data justifiable?
  • Does authors’ interpretation of the data fully reach the conclusion?

Take-home msg

After reading a paper, summarization of your own take-home message is always most critical

  • Try to use 1-5 bullet points to summarize what you learn from the paper
  • Techniques that can be used in your own research
  • Mistakes or lessons to be learn

Enhance

Resources: As a reviewer

Pretend you are a reviewer and come up with one critique of the paper (e.g., something the authors could have done better, or an experiment that would strengthen the paper).

Your decision?

Pretend this paper is not published yet?

  • What category will you fill in the decision letter? (e.g., Excellent, minor concerns, major concerns & rejection)

Propose new experiment?

Propose experiments that

  • Directly address the gap of the conclusion authors made
  • Experiments that may explain the unexpected results
  • Experiments that will make the paper generally better

Request new analyses?

The analyses can be done better with the current dataset.

  • e.g., The author should use K-W test instead of one-way ANOVA

Writings

  • The Flow?

    Is the flow of the writing good enough? How can they improve?

    • e.g., Figures and corresponding main texts may need to be swapped to better help the flow of the paper
    • e.g., Some figures are less important and can be put in supplementary data

  • Thorough discussion?

    Do they have thorough discussion of their interpretation and conclusion?

  • Missing citations?

    In the introduction or discussion, have they missed critical citation in the field?

Data/Method

Transparency & reproducibility

Nowadays, most research journals require that data/methods should be transparent and reproducible:

  • Please take a look at the Nature reporting standard nature.com/nature-portfolio/editorial-policies/reporting-standards

  • Examples to check:

    • availability of data (Are the RNA-seq data submitted to public domain like GEO?)
    • code/algorithm availability (Are the codes uploaded in Github or any other code repository?)
    • decent method/material description for other lab to reproduce

Identify your knowledge gap

What are your areas of confusion as you analyze this paper?  Are there terms that you don’t understand? What questions do you have about the research aim, methods or data?
